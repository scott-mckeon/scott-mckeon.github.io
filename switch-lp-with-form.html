No catch

Straight up about how this works.

Here's the honest version, because you'd ask anyway:

  • We compare, you decide. Homerun Switch lines up electricity and gas plans from Australian retailers so you can see what's available and how it compares to what you're on now.
  • We can't promise you'll save money. There are clear regulations around promising energy savings - and rightly so. Whether you end up on a better plan depends on your current retailer, where you live and how you use energy. We show you the options. What you do with that is up to you.
  • We may earn a commission if you switch. If you choose to switch through us, we may receive a fee from the retailer. It doesn't affect which plans we show you or how they're ranked - and it helps us keep building products that make sharehouse life easier.
  • We're not an energy retailer. Homerun is an Australian app for running a sharehouse. We don't generate or sell energy.

What counts as life support equipment?

According to the National Energy Retail Rules and the Victorian Energy Retail Code, life support equipment includes:

  • Oxygen concentrator
  • Intermittent peritoneal dialysis machine
  • Kidney dialysis machine
  • Chronic positive airways pressure respirator (CPAP)
  • Crigler-Najjar syndrome phototherapy equipment
  • Ventilator for life support
  • Any other equipment certified by a registered medical practitioner as required for life support at the property - including where a current medical certificate confirms a person's medical condition requires continued supply of electricity or gas

This may also include: external heart pumps, respirators (iron lung), suction pumps, feeding pumps, insulin pumps, airbed vibrators, hot water, nebulizers/humidifiers/vaporizers, apnoea monitors, medically required heating or air conditioning, medically required refrigeration, and powered wheelchairs.
